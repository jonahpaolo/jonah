Lou Marxis Gallevo

Mr. Lou Marxis Gallevo is an educator, leader, and advocate. He is currently an International Educator teaching under the International Baccalaureate Program (IB World Schools) and International General Certifcate of Secondary Education (IGCSE- Cambridge) at the Mentari intercultural School Bintaro- Indonesia. Mr. Gallevo has six years teaching experience under K-12 curriculum teaching Araling Panlipunan from Grade 6-12 at Elizabeth Seton School where he became the Supreme Student Council Adviser for 3 years. This stemmed from his training at the Philippine Normal University-Manila where he graduated as Cum Laude. He is also a member of Una Mano Aiuta L Altra Inc, a Non-government Organization providing educational and spiritual support for the children of Tondo, Manila. His advocacy for transformative education has reached Indonesia as he is a regular facilitator and speaker for Public School Teachers of Indonesia under the Yayasan Emmanuel’s Innovative School’s Program in partnership with the Indonesia’s Ministry of Education.

Aubrey Mikaela Joson

Ms. Aubrey Mikaela Joson completed her Bachelor’s degree in Social Science Education with a Certificate in Teaching Junior High School from the Philippine Normal University in 2018, graduating as a top student and Cum Laude. She has since dedicated herself to teaching Araling Panlipunan for the past six years. Currently, Ms. Joson teaches Araling Panlipunan at Valenzuela National High School, where she also serves as the Supreme Secondary Learner Government (SSLG) Adviser. Recognized for her exceptional contributions to education, Ms. Joson was named one of the Outstanding Teachers of SDO Valenzuela for both 2021 and 2022. She is also an active volunteer and resource speaker, she frequently addresses topics such as youth leadership, human rights, child protection policy, gender equality, and volunteerism.

Mel Franky S. Lizardo

Mel Franky Lizardo is a dedicated educator with a Bachelor’s degree in Social Science Education from the Philippine Normal University. From 2018 to 2023, Mel worked as a Licensed Professional Teacher at San Sebastian College Recoletos Manila, and is currently an academic course designer. Pursuing further education, Mel is studying for an MA in Anthropology at the University of the Philippines Diliman. Between 2017 and 2019, Mel was actively involved in Project Gala, leading impactful initiatives such as Unang GALA in Manila and the Iloveyou300 project in Itogon, Benguet. Passionate about teaching and volunteer work, Mel excels in organizing logistics, managing budgets, and creating audiovisual and graphic materials to support educational endeavors.
